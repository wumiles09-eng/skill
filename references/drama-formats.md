# Short Drama Format Specifications

## The Fundamental Formula: Hook + Reversal + Cliffhanger

Every single episode follows this ironclad structure. No exceptions.

```
[0-3s]   HOOK: Visual shock, provocative line, or dramatic reversal
[3-15s]  SETUP: Establish scene context and character immediate goal
[15-45s] CONFRONTATION: Conflict escalation, tension building, stakes raised
[45-55s] TWIST: New information, reversal, or escalation
[55-60s] CLIFFHANGER: Unresolved tension that FORCES next episode click
```

## The 6-Second Law

**80%+ of users decide whether to continue watching within the first 6 seconds.**

This is not hyperbole — it's platform data from TikTok for Business. Your first frame must:
- Raise a question
- Show a conflict
- Create visual intrigue
- Or present a provocative image

**Bad hook**: Establishing shot of a building, peaceful morning routine
**Good hook**: A glass shattering, a slap mid-motion, "I know what you did"

## The Golden Triangle (Episode 1 Requirement)

From Hongguo/Redfruit methodology — within Episode 1, establish:
1. **Identity**: Who is the protagonist? (current status, not backstory)
2. **Crisis**: What immediate threat do they face?
3. **Goal**: What do they want? (clear, active, driving)

If these three are not clear within Episode 1, rewrite the opening.

## Episode Structure (60-80 Episodes)

### Runtime Specifications
- **Per episode**: 60-90 seconds
- **Total series**: 60-80 minutes (equivalent to feature film)
- **Hook placement**: First 3 seconds
- **Cliffhanger**: Last 5-10 seconds

### The "Card" System (付费卡点)

Short dramas use a "card" payment system:
- **First Card (一卡)**: Episodes 1-10 (free preview + first payment point)
- **Second Card (二卡)**: Episodes 11-20 (second payment unlock)
- **Third Card (三卡)**: Episodes 21-30 (third payment unlock)
- Continue pattern through episode 80

**Critical**: The episode immediately BEFORE each payment point must have the STRONGEST cliffhanger in that arc. This is where conversion happens.

### TikTok for Business Golden Formula for Ad Creatives

When episodes are cut into promotional ad materials:

| Phase | Timing | Goal | Technique |
|-------|--------|------|-----------|
| **Initial attraction** | 0-6 seconds | Stop the scroll | High visual shock, conflict, or attractive cast |
| **Secondary retention** | 6s-2min | Keep them watching | Intensify character conflict, plot reversal |
| **Conversion hook** | 2min+ | Drive download/purchase | Abrupt cliffhanger + CTA button |

Key metrics for hit ad creatives:
- **6s completion rate**: ≥20% (hit series threshold)
- **CTR**: ≥1.5%
- **3-day ad spend**: ≥$20,000

### Series Arc Structure

**Episodes 1-5**: Setup & Hook
- Ep 1: Inciting incident in first 30 seconds. End with "What happens now?"
- Ep 2-3: Core conflict established. End each with escalation.
- Ep 4-5: First mini-crisis. Episode 5 ends with FIRST MAJOR TWIST.

**Episodes 6-20**: Escalation (First Card)
- Complications pile up rapidly
- Antagonist demonstrates power
- Romantic tension builds (if applicable)
- Each episode ends with escalation
- Episode 10: First genre set piece (gala, confrontation, revelation)
- Episode 15: Mid-first-card crisis
- Episode 20: END OF FIRST CARD — strongest cliffhanger yet

**Episodes 21-40**: Deepening Crisis (Second Card)
- Protagonist's lowest emotional point
- Secret in danger of exposure
- Relationship at breaking point
- False resolution that backfires
- Episode 30: Major set piece (public event, trial, wedding)
- Episode 40: END OF SECOND CARD — devastating cliffhanger

**Episodes 41-60**: Reversal & Rise (Third Card)
- Protagonist fights back with new strength
- Hidden powers/identities revealed
- Alliances shift
- Karma moments for antagonists begin
- Episode 50: Major turning point set piece
- Episode 60: END OF THIRD CARD — everything changes

**Episodes 61-75**: Climax Build
- Final confrontation setup
- All subplots converging
- Emotional payoffs begin
- Romantic resolution (if applicable)
- Episode 70: Pre-finale set piece

**Episodes 76-80**: Resolution
- Final victory — protagonist wins on her own terms
- Epilogue with happy/stable future
- Last 5 seconds: Optional sequel hook

## Pacing Rules

1. **Never resolve conflict in the same episode it appears** (unless it's a fake resolution)
2. **Every 5 episodes must have a major escalation** (mid-season twist)
3. **Every 10 episodes must have a genre-defining set piece** (gala, trial, wedding, boardroom battle)
4. **Romantic leads must have meaningful interaction every 3-5 episodes minimum**
5. **No episode without a hook and cliffhanger**
6. **One new piece of information per episode minimum**
7. **Emotional beat must land before episode ends** — if she's sad, we feel it; if she's triumphant, we cheer
8. **Every episode needs 1-2 plot reversals** (per 2025 industry data — ~2 reversals per episode is standard for US market)

## Episode Count by Genre

| Genre | Recommended Episodes | Reason |
|-------|---------------------|--------|
| Romance / Contract Marriage | 60-70 | Tighter emotional arc |
| Revenge / Underdog | 70-80 | Need space for escalation |
| Mystery / Thriller | 60-75 | Risk of plot fatigue |
| Family Drama | 70-80 | Multiple subplots |
| Workplace / Corporate | 60-70 | Professional pacing |
| Supernatural (Werewolf/Vampire) | 70-80 | Lore + romance need room |

## Scene Construction Within Episodes

Each 60-90 second episode contains 2-4 scenes. Every scene must:

1. **Move the plot forward** OR **deepen character relationship**
2. **End with a question** — verbal or visual
3. **Contain one emotional beat** — anger, hope, fear, triumph, betrayal
4. **Be visually interesting** — not just two people talking in a room

**Scene transition technique**: Use "SMASH CUT TO:" between scenes to maintain energy. Match visual elements across cuts (a slammed door → a opened drawer; a tear → a raindrop).

## The "1-2 Reversals Per Episode" Standard

Based on 2025 US market data (广大大 / DataEye), every episode should contain 1-2 plot reversals:

- **Reversal 1** (mid-episode): New information that changes the scene's direction
- **Reversal 2** (end-episode): The cliffhanger — often contradicting what viewer expected

Example structure:
```
Setup: She's about to sign the contract →
Reversal 1: She reads the fine print and stops →
Climax: She confronts the other party →
Reversal 2 (Cliffhanger): He reveals he knew she'd read it — this was a test
```
