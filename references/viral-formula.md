# The Viral Formula: Hooks, Pay Points & Binge Architecture

## The Golden Formula (爆款黄金公式)

Based on TikTok for Business data and industry best practices:

```
VIRAL POTENTIAL = HOOK STRENGTH x CONFLICT DENSITY x CLIFFHANGER PAIN
```

Each variable scores 1-10. A score below 6 in any category means the episode needs rewriting.

## The 6-Second Law: Hook Design

### What Works (ranked by effectiveness)

| Hook Type | Example | Effectiveness |
|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Physical shock | Slap mid-swing, glass shattering | 10/10 |
| Verbal bomb | "I know what you did" / "You're fired" | 9/10 |
| Visual contradiction | Homeless person in penthouse, billionaire in dumpster | 9/10 |
| Question that demands answer | "She died three years ago. So who's that woman?" | 9/10 |
| Sudden reversal | Wedding kiss → cuts to divorce papers | 8/10 |
| Powerful entrance | Car screeches up, doors open, all eyes turn | 8/10 |
| Intimate interruption | Almost-kiss → door bursts open | 8/10 |
| Time jump shock | "Six months later. She's unrecognizable." | 7/10 |

### What Fails

- Peaceful establishing shots
- Character thinking silently
- "Three months earlier..." without context
- Waking up, getting dressed, normal routine
- Two people having a calm conversation

### Hook Templates by Genre

**Billionaire/Contract Marriage**:
- "Sign it." / "What's the catch?" / "You have to marry me. Tonight."
- Pre-nup on table, pen hovering, phone rings with bad news

**Revenge/Glow-Up**:
- "You won't recognize her." / "She's back." / Glass shatters at reunion
- Fat/before photo morphs to stunning entrance

**Werewolf/Alpha**:
- "I reject you." / Wolf eyes flash in darkness / "She's not who you think she is."
- Full moon, howl, transformation mid-scream

**Identity Swap**:
- "That's not your name." / Twin side-by-side, one in rags, one in riches
- "If anyone finds out, we're both dead."

**Workplace**:
- "You're fired." / "The promotion goes to..." / Presentation stolen mid-pitch
- "I built this. And I'm taking it back."

## The Card System: Paid Conversion Architecture

### Understanding the Payment Psychology

Users pay to resolve tension. Your job is to create tension that feels unbearable to leave unresolved.

```
CARD STRUCTURE:

Free Preview (Ep 1-5):     Strongest hook in series → builds investment
First Card (Ep 6-10):      $X.99 unlock → cliffhanger at Ep 10 must be DEVASTATING
Second Card (Ep 11-20):    $X.99 unlock → Ep 20 is identity/relationship crisis
Third Card (Ep 21-30):     $X.99 unlock → Ep 30 is public showdown
Fourth Card (Ep 31-40):    $X.99 unlock → Ep 40 is betrayal/loss
Fifth Card (Ep 41-50):     $X.99 unlock → Ep 50 is transformation
Sixth Card (Ep 51-60):     $X.99 unlock → Ep 60 is reversal
Seventh Card (Ep 61-70):   $X.99 unlock → Ep 70 is pre-finale
Eighth Card (Ep 71-80):    $X.99 unlock → resolution
```

### The Conversion Formula

For each card boundary, ensure:

1. **Unanswered question** that specifically demands the NEXT episode
2. **Emotional investment** — we care what happens to this person
3. **Time pressure** — something will happen if they don't act
4. **Personal stake** — not abstract, but personally meaningful

**Bad card cliffhanger**: "Will she sign the contract?" (Who cares?)
**Good card cliffhanger**: "She reaches for the pen — and sees her dead husband's signature already on the page." (What?! How?!)

## Conflict Density: The Engine of Binge

### Measuring Conflict Density

Count the number of conflicts per episode:

| Conflict Count | Episode Feel | Binge Likelihood |
|---------------|-------------|------------------|
| 0-1 | Boring | Abandon after 1 episode |
| 2 | Slow | Watch 2-3, then quit |
| 3 | Good | Watch 5+ |
| 4+ | Addictive | Binge entire series |

**Target: 3-4 conflicts per episode, escalating in intensity.**

### Types of Conflict to Layer

1. **External plot conflict** (will she get the promotion?)
2. **Relationship conflict** (will they admit their feelings?)
3. **Internal conflict** (can she trust again?)
4. **Social conflict** (will the family accept her?)
5. **Time conflict** (deadline approaching)

### The Conflict Sandwich

Every episode should layer conflicts like this:

```
[External conflict opens] → [Relationship complication] → [External escalates] 
  → [Internal struggle] → [External climax + cliffhanger]
```

Example:
- She has to present at the board meeting (external)
- But her ex is on the board (relationship)
- The presentation file is corrupted (external escalates)
- She's having a panic attack but can't show it (internal)
- Ex stands up as she's about to speak — "I have something to say first" (cliffhanger)

## The Binge Loop: Making Episode N+1 Irresistible

### The 3-Second Rule

After episode N ends, the viewer has 3 seconds to decide: click next or leave.

Your cliffhanger must:
1. **Create a question** that demands an answer
2. **Trigger an emotion** that wants resolution
3. **Feel personal** to the viewer's investment

### Binge Architecture Pattern

```
Episode N:
  - Setup: Protagonist prepares for important meeting
  - Conflict: Antagonist sabotages preparation
  - Escalation: Meeting moved earlier, no time to fix
  - Cliffhanger: Walks into room, sees betrayer sitting at head of table

Episode N+1 (must resolve AND escalate):
  - Immediate: She handles the surprise (competence shown)
  - Twist: Betrayer reveals new information that changes everything
  - Escalation: Now she has leverage, but using it risks exposure
  - Cliffhanger: Pulls out phone to make the call that will change everything

Episode N+2:
  - Immediate: The call goes wrong/unexpected result
  - Twist: Someone she trusts overheard
  - Escalation: Trust is broken at the worst moment
  - Cliffhanger: "I know what you did. And I'm going to tell everyone."
```

## The "Karma Moment" Design

The karma moment is the emotional peak of the entire series — when the antagonist gets their comeuppance.

### Karma Moment Formula

```
KARMA IMPACT = SETUP EFFORT x PUBLIC HUMILIATION x POETIC JUSTICE
```

**Setup effort**: The more the protagonist suffered to set this up, the sweeter it is.
**Public humiliation**: Must be WITNESSED by people whose opinion matters to the antagonist.
**Poetic justice**: The method of downfall should mirror their crime.

### Karma Moment Templates

| Antagonist Crime | Poetic Justice | Setting |
|-----------------|---------------|---------|
| Stole her company | She takes theirs | Boardroom, shareholders present |
| Publicly humiliated her | She exposes their secret | Same venue as original humiliation |
| Used her for money | She proves she never needed it | Charity auction where they bid against her |
| Lied about her reputation | She livestreams the truth | Social media viral moment |
| Cheated with her partner | She walks in with someone better | Their engagement party |
| Sabotaged her career | She becomes their boss | First day at new company — their company |

## The "2 Reversals Per Episode" Standard (2025 US Market)

Based on current US market data, every episode should contain 1-2 reversals:
- **Mid-episode reversal**: New info changes scene direction
- **End-episode reversal**: The cliffhanger — contradicts viewer expectation

This density is what separates addictive series from forgettable ones.
