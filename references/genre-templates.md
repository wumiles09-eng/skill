# Genre Templates & Story Patterns

## Genre Selection Guide

| Market | Top Performing Genres | Emerging / Avoid |
|--------|----------------------|-----------------|
| North America | Werewolf/Alpha, Billionaire Romance, Vampire, Comeback | Mafia (emerging), Systems/Sci-Fi (avoid), Son-in-law (avoid) |
| Southeast Asia | CEO Romance, Contract Marriage, Revenge | Family Drama, Campus Romance |
| Europe | Billionaire, Vampire, Workplace | Historical, Mystery |
| Japan | Family drama, female empowerment, emotional | (Localized content preferred) |

**Note for US market**: Werewolf/Alpha is the #1 genre. If uncertain, start here. Billionaire romance is #2. Both share the same emotional structure with different window dressing.

**Avoid for US market**: System/golden finger plots (系统流), pure sci-fi premises, son-in-law/live-in husband narratives (赘婿), pure xianxia/cultivation without modern wrapper. DataEye 2025 H1 shows these have low international acceptance.

## 1. The Billionaire's Secret (Contract Marriage)

**Premise**: Two strangers enter a marriage contract for mutual benefit; genuine feelings develop.

**American Adaptation Points**:
- Business merger / inheritance clause / green card (plausible American reasons)
- Pre-nup negotiations as chemistry showcase
- Workplace proximity creates forced interaction
- Ex-fiancée returns as antagonist
- **US nuance**: The billionaire must have a *wound* that explains his emotional unavailability. American audiences reject "rich and therefore desirable" — he must earn her love through personal growth.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Contract signed under pressure. Both have hidden agendas.
- Ep 5: First forced public appearance as couple. Sparks fly.
- Ep 10: Gala set piece — they must perform as loving couple. Almost kiss.
- Ep 15: Accidental intimate moment. One feels something; other denies it.
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — contract threatened, truth exposed
- Ep 25: One falls first, other doesn't trust it. Emotional walls up.
- Ep 30: Trial set piece — legal or social test of their "marriage"
- Ep 35: Deepening intimacy. Letting guards down.
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — secret agenda revealed, betrayal
- Ep 45: Separation / misunderstanding. Both suffer.
- Ep 50: Transformation set piece — one or both fundamentally change
- Ep 55: External threat forces them back together
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — final obstacle before love
- Ep 65-70: Reconciliation building to genuine commitment
- Ep 75: Grand gesture — not money, but vulnerability
- Ep 80: Genuine marriage / commitment. Happy ending.

## 2. The Return (Revenge / Glow-Up)

**Premise**: Protagonist returns transformed after being wronged; systematically destroys enemies.

**American Adaptation Points**:
- Weight loss / style transformation / wealth acquisition
- Former bullies now in vulnerable positions
- Public exposure via social media / viral video
- Professional domain expertise as weapon
- **US nuance**: The revenge must be *earned through competence*, not just luck or hidden identity. She must be better at their jobs, smarter in public, and more successful. American audiences want to see her WORK for her revenge.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Flashback to humiliation; return entrance in new form
- Ep 5: First encounter; antagonist doesn't recognize them
- Ep 10: Gala set piece — enters their world, turns heads
- Ep 15: First revenge move; public humiliation of one bully
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — identity almost exposed
- Ep 25: Antagonist realizes who they are; panics
- Ep 30: Trial set piece — professional showdown, she wins
- Ep 35: Romance begins with someone from new life (complicates revenge)
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — past collides with present
- Ep 45: Allies from past return to help
- Ep 50: Transformation complete — fully empowered
- Ep 55: Main antagonist's downfall begins
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — choice: revenge or happiness?
- Ep 65-70: Final confrontation; karma delivered
- Ep 75: Public victory; reputation restored
- Ep 80: New beginning; peace achieved, love secured

## 3. Wrong Place, Right Person (Identity Swap / Impersonation)

**Premise**: Protagonist takes another's identity and finds unexpected love and purpose.

**American Adaptation Points**:
- Twin separation / witness protection / lookalike switch
- Class contrast (rich/poor) drives fish-out-of-water comedy
- Genuine identity crisis and moral ambiguity
- Threat of exposure creates ticking clock
- **US nuance**: American audiences need the protagonist to *choose* to reveal the truth, not just be caught. The moral weight of the lie must be felt. The love interest must choose her real self over the fake identity.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Swap established under pressure. Both have reasons.
- Ep 5: First day in new life. Close calls, funny mistakes.
- Ep 10: Gala set piece — almost exposed in public
- Ep 15: Falling for someone who doesn't know real them
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — lie threatens to unravel
- Ep 25: Real identity's life is better than expected; torn
- Ep 30: Trial set piece — must perform skills she doesn't have
- Ep 35: Growing guilt; considering confession
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — discovery by love interest; betrayal felt
- Ep 45: Forced to maintain lie by external threat
- Ep 50: Transformation — actually becomes the person she's pretending to be
- Ep 55: Love interest suspects; tests her
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — truth comes out publicly
- Ep 65-70: Must prove genuine connection; real self is enough
- Ep 75: Accepted for who she is, not identity
- Ep 80: New authentic life; love based on truth

## 4. The Underdog's Rise (Workplace / Talent)

**Premise**: Overlooked employee/talent fights systemic bias and proves their worth.

**American Adaptation Points**:
- Corporate glass ceiling / nepotism / old boys' club
- Meritocracy belief resonates strongly
- Competition / pitch / trial as set pieces
- Mentorship from unexpected source
- **US nuance**: The victory must be *demonstrably fair*. She must be genuinely better, not just luckier. American audiences hate "participation trophy" wins. She should outperform them in a measurable way.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Passed over for undeserving rival. Anger ignites.
- Ep 5: Threatened with firing; makes bold promise
- Ep 10: First opportunity to prove worth; partially blocked
- Ep 15: Gains first ally; mentor appears
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — major project at risk
- Ep 25: Shows competence; wins small victory
- Ep 30: Pitch/competition set piece — rival cheats, she adapts
- Ep 35: Romance with colleague complicates workplace dynamics
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — project stolen, blamed
- Ep 45: Lowest point — fired or publicly shamed
- Ep 50: Resurfaces with evidence / better solution
- Ep 55: Public demonstration of superiority
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — final confrontation with antagonist
- Ep 65-70: Professional vindication; reputation restored
- Ep 75: Promotion / ownership / independence achieved
- Ep 80: Running her own show; love and success

## 5. Love After Loss (Second Chance Romance)

**Premise**: Widow/divorcée rebuilds life and finds unexpected new love.

**American Adaptation Points**:
- Late 30s-40s protagonist (targets demographic directly)
- Children as plot device and emotional anchor
- Grief handled with respect; not rushed
- New love interest challenges isolation
- **US nuance**: This genre directly targets the core demographic (women 25-45). The children must be real characters with agency, not just props. The grief arc must be genuine — she doesn't "get over it," she "learns to live alongside it."

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Content but closed off; routine established
- Ep 5: New person disrupts stability; she's annoyed
- Ep 10: Social event set piece — forced interaction
- Ep 15: First date / first laugh since loss; guilt follows
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — secret from past threatens
- Ep 25: Guilt about moving on; steps back
- Ep 30: Crisis requires support; reluctantly accepts help
- Ep 35: Children begin to approve / facilitate
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — ex's memory vs. new love
- Ep 45: External threat (ex returns / custody / family objection)
- Ep 50: Transformation — redefines what happiness means
- Ep 55: Chooses to risk heart again
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — past fully confronted
- Ep 65-70: Integration — honoring past while embracing future
- Ep 75: Proposal / commitment that respects her journey
- Ep 80: New family forged; peace with past

## 6. The Hidden Heir (Secret Identity / Royalty)

**Premise**: Ordinary protagonist discovers they're heir to massive fortune/legacy.

**American Adaptation Points**:
- DNA test / letter from estranged parent / trust fund activation
- Sudden wealth with strings attached
- Existing family resents their existence
- Fish-out-of-water in high society
- **US nuance**: American audiences want to see her EARN her place, not just inherit it. The "old money" family should underestimate her, and she should prove her worth through street smarts, work ethic, or genuine kindness. The victory is integration on her terms, not assimilation into their world.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Discovery scene; disbelief
- Ep 5: First encounter with new family; hostility
- Ep 10: Gala set piece — humiliated by "real" family
- Ep 15: Attempt to fit in; cultural clashes
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — inheritance threatened
- Ep 25: Meets ally/love interest outside family
- Ep 30: Legal/business test — she proves unexpectedly capable
- Ep 35: Growing confidence; stopped trying to fit in
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — betrayal by someone trusted
- Ep 45: Forced to choose: old life or new?
- Ep 50: Transformation — stops playing by their rules
- Ep 55: Uses "ordinary" skills to solve "elite" problem
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — inheritance on the line
- Ep 65-70: Claims rightful place on own terms
- Ep 75: Family acknowledges her worth
- Ep 80: Builds bridge between two worlds; happy ending

## 7. Fated to the Alpha (Werewolf / Supernatural Romance)

**Why This Works in America**: This is the #1 performing genre on ReelShort. It combines:
- Supernatural "fated bond" that justifies instant chemistry
- Pack hierarchy that mirrors corporate power structures
- Physical transformation as emotional metaphor
- The "rejection → regret" arc that empowers the female lead

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: First shift/encounter; fated bond recognized
- Ep 5: Alpha rejects bond (pride/power/past wound); she flees
- Ep 10: Pack gathering set piece — public rejection, humiliation
- Ep 15: She begins transformation (physical/emotional/empowerment)
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — rival female threatens; pack in danger
- Ep 25: She saves someone using new abilities; Alpha notices
- Ep 30: Full moon / pack trial set piece — she proves herself
- Ep 35: Alpha begins pursuing; she's not interested anymore
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — rival exposes secret; pack turns on her
- Ep 45: She leaves pack; Alpha realizes what he lost
- Ep 50: Transformation complete — powerful in her own right
- Ep 55: External threat (rogue pack / hunters) forces reunion
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — she saves the pack; Alpha begs
- Ep 65-70: She chooses whether to accept bond — on HER terms
- Ep 75: New power structure; she becomes Luna/co-Alpha
- Ep 80: Pack united; bond fully accepted as equals

**Critical Rules**:
- She must CHOOSE the bond, not just accept destiny
- The rejection must wound her deeply enough to drive transformation
- Pack politics must be clear and logical (who has power, why)
- Physical transformation must mirror emotional growth
- The "regret" moment must be genuine and prolonged — he must suffer

## 8. Immortal Desires (Vampire Romance)

**Premise**: A mortal woman falls for a centuries-old vampire, navigating immortality, blood bonds, and supernatural danger.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: Mysterious encounter; he's drawn to her inexplicably
- Ep 5: She discovers his nature; fear and fascination
- Ep 10: Masquerade/gala set piece — vampire society exposed
- Ep 15: First blood bond / intimate moment
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — vampire hunters close in
- Ep 25: She must choose: mortal life or his world
- Ep 30: Council trial set piece — they judge her worthiness
- Ep 35: Deepening bond; she starts changing
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — rival vampire threatens
- Ep 45: Forced separation for her safety
- Ep 50: Transformation — she becomes something new (not full vampire)
- Ep 55: She returns, stronger, saves him for once
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — final battle with ancient enemy
- Ep 65-70: Victory; chooses her path (mortal with him / hybrid / turned)
- Ep 75: New equilibrium in vampire world
- Ep 80: Eternal love accepted; found family complete

## 9. Dangerous Vows (Mafia / Dark Romance)

**Premise**: A woman enters the orbit of a dangerous man for protection or survival; finds unexpected connection in the darkness.

**Why This Works**: Appeals to the "forbidden attraction" fantasy with high stakes. The danger is external (enemies, police, rival families) creating constant tension.

**Key Beats**:
- Ep 1: She needs protection; he's the only option
- Ep 5: First encounter — he's cold, dismissive; she's terrified but defiant
- Ep 10: Public event set piece — she plays a role; he notices
- Ep 15: First "soft" moment — he shows vulnerability
- Ep 20: FIRST CARD CLIFFHANGER — rival family attacks; she's in danger
- Ep 25: He protects her; she sees the man behind the mask
- Ep 30: Undercover/infiltration set piece — they work together
- Ep 35: Trust building; she learns his wounds
- Ep 40: SECOND CARD CLIFFHANGER — betrayal from within
- Ep 45: Separated; both in danger
- Ep 50: She proves capable — saves herself AND him
- Ep 55: Reunion; he acknowledges her strength
- Ep 60: THIRD CARD CLIFFHANGER — final family war
- Ep 65-70: Victory; they choose each other and "out"
- Ep 75: New beginning — legitimate life or new empire
- Ep 80: Dangerous love secured; equals in crime and love

**Critical Rules**:
- She must have her own competence/skills — not just a damsel
- The "dark" elements must stay in fantasy realm
- Clear line between fictional trope and real criminal glorification
- She must actively choose him, not be swept along
- The "out" must feel earned — they work for their happiness

## Micro-Innovation Strategies (Baiyang Studio Method)

For any genre, apply these micro-innovations to avoid stale execution:

### Character Micro-Innovation
Add ONE unexpected trait to a familiar archetype:
- CEO billionaire + severe social anxiety = vulnerable power
- Revenge heroine + comedy as weapon = witty avenger
- Sweet innocent + secret hacker = hidden competence

### Setting Micro-Innovation
Move familiar dynamics to unexpected settings:
- Contract marriage → family-owned vineyard
- Office rivalry → startup incubator
- Secret identity → witness protection program
- Enemies to lovers → rival food trucks

### Expression Style Innovation
Same trope, new delivery:
- Comedy-drama hybrid (The Office meets contract marriage)
- Dual timeline (past/present parallel)
- Reverse chronology (start at the end)

**The Core Rule**: "Old wine in new bottles" — the emotional beats are ancient; what differentiates is the packaging.
